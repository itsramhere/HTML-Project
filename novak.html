What is a grandslam?
The four Grand Slam tournaments in tennis are the sport’s most prestigious events.
Played on different surfaces, they test players’ versatility and skill. Here is a list of the name of grandslams, the countries they're hosted in and the type of surface they are played on:
Name Country Month of conduction Surface
Australian Open Australia January Hard court
French Open France May Clay court
Wimbledon UK June / July Grass
US Open USA September Hard court

What is a Masters 1000 event?
The ATP Masters 1000 events are nine elite tennis tournaments held annually, just below Grand Slams in prestige. Featuring top-ranked players, they offer valuable ranking points and global exposure.
Played on various surfaces worldwide, these events test consistency, adaptability, and endurance, making them crucial in shaping the men’s professional tennis season.

Novak Djokovic at Grand Slam events in the year 2023:

The Australian Open

Novak Djokovic won the 2023 Australian Open, defeating Stefanos Tsitsipas in straight sets. It marked his 10th title in Melbourne and 22nd Grand Slam overall, tying Rafael Nadal’s record. Battling a hamstring injury, he showed resilience and dominance, reclaiming the World No. 1 ranking and solidifying his case to be called the Greatest of All Time.

The French Open alias Roland Garros

Novak Djokovic won the 2023 Roland Garros, defeating Casper Ruud in the final. He claimed his 23rd Grand Slam title, surpassing Rafael Nadal’s record. Displaying exceptional skill and mental strength, Djokovic became the first man to win all four Grand Slams at least three times, reaffirming his place in tennis history.


At this level the last thing you want is cramps and physical problems at the late stages of the grand slams. I feel for him, I feel sorry, I hope he can recover and come back pretty soon.

- Novak Djokovic

Wimbledon

At the 2023 Wimbledon Championships, Novak Djokovic reached the final but lost to Carlos Alcaraz in a thrilling five-set match. Despite the defeat, Djokovic showcased his trademark resilience and skill throughout the tournament. His performance reaffirmed his elite status, as he continued to challenge younger rivals on tennis’s biggest stage.

US OPEN

Novak Djokovic won the 2023 US Open, defeating Daniil Medvedev in the final. It was his 24th (and last) Grand Slam title, extending his record. Dominating the tournament with composure and precision, Djokovic cemented his legacy as the most successful men’s tennis player in history, showcasing enduring excellence at age 36.

The Masters 1000 Events

Novak Djokovic played in only 6 of the 9 Masters 1000 events. Here is the breakdown of each of them:

Monte-Carlo Masters

Djokovic was eliminated in the third round of the 2023 Monte-Carlo Masters, falling to Lorenzo Musetti in straight sets.

Madrid Open

Djokovic reached the quarterfinals of the 2023 Madrid Open but was defeated by Carlos Alcaraz in a closely contested match.

Italian Open (Rome)

As the defending champion, Djokovic was ousted in the quarterfinals of the 2023 Italian Open by Holger Rune, who went on to win the title.

Cincinnati Masters

Djokovic clinched his 39th Masters 1000 title at the 2023 Cincinnati Masters, defeating Carlos Alcaraz in a historic final that lasted nearly four hours. It is safe to say that that was an instant classic.

Paris Masters

Djokovic secured his record-extending seventh Paris Masters title in 2023, defeating Grigor Dimitrov 6–4, 6–3 in the final.

ATP Finals (Turin)

Djokovic reached the semifinals of the 2023 ATP Finals in Turin but was eliminated by eventual champion Daniil Medvedev.
